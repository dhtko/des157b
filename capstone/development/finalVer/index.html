Garbage Is Piling Up...

Ever since the industrial revolution, our world has entered an era of fast production: mass production has become the industry standard, and manufacturers produce massive products. As such, industries are constantly releasing new products, and people can access any product they want anytime.

However, as fast production has become the new standard over the years, several economic problems have started to rise. The first notable problem is that too many products have been released over the years. This became a notable environmental problem because the leftover stocks have become waste that cannot be recycled from using non-biodegradable materials like plastic. Furthermore, a new trend called 'fast fashion,' where people quickly replace their products (especially noted in the fashion-related products), has boosted the production rate as well.

In addition to the increase in leftover stocks, the rate of recyclability is relatively low. Despite the numerous ways to consume materials being available, recyclability is the most important because other methods, like incineration, harm our environment by emitting toxic gases. However, not many countries are active with the technique because of its low efficiency (it takes too much time to deal with the massive amount of garbage).
